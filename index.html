Welcome!

Our laboratory aims to establish engineering capabilities for contributing future societies through utilization of invisible subsurface environments and resources. Especially, our main targets are:

1. Securing supplements of energy, resources, and water;

2. Developing and utilizing subsurface information;

3. Managing/protecting the subsurface environment for sustainable society.

Left hand side photo shows the lab members at Spring 2018 in the balcony of Environment Building, Kashiwa Campus, The University of Tokyo.

Current researches use the best approaches to handle a wide variety of space/time dimensions with respects to the subsurface environments. Multidisciplines are employed in our laboratory:

[1] Migration and fate of groundwater/solute;

[2] Long-term environmental change and/or stability of subsurface;

[3] Important functions of subsurface fluids in geodynamics.

Right hand side photo was taken at the Kujukuri beach for a field estimation of groundwater elevation

Every year we have field excursions to discover the interesting natural phenomenon.

In 2017, we visited the historical dams,and geothermal power stations, etc., in Akita prefecture.

In 2015, 2013, 2012, we visited Qifu prefecture, Togichi prefecture and Hakone, Izu, separately.
