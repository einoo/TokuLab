Evaluation of groundwater environment and its long-term stability in coastal area

To evaluate the long-term(about 10,000 years) stability of surface and subsurface environment, we try to construct the methodology integrating results from interdisciplinary approach including topography, geology, hydrology, archaeology, etc. For example, we develop and confirm techniques to reconstruct glacial sea level change from records on sediment with high resolution in the field. By another approach, we recently succeeded to observe the quantity and quality of submarine groundwater discharge(SGD). We now try to evaluate whole groundwater systems from inland to ocean area.

Development of methods for evaluating the physical properties of underground formations from observation on natural fractuation

It is important to obtain the physical property of underground materials for sustainable development. For this evaluation, we are developing the techniques from the view point of following ideas. Geosphere responses to natural fractuation such as tidal loading, atmospheric pressure change and seasonal recharge change, etc. Many studies considered these signals as the noise against the focused phenomena and removed them for their analysis. However, there is the probability to evaluate the physical properties of underground materials under the natural condition by analyzing these signals with poromechanics theory. Now we are developing and applying the observation equipments for this approach in the several fields. These approach are expected to become one of the general methods to evaluate the physical properties of underground materials under the natural condition.

Evaluation of groundwater contamination and development of remediation techniques

Groundwater and soil contamination problems are big issues for environment and our health. Especially, the contamination by compounds including hydrocarbon or chlorohydrocarbon are important. We are developing the experiment to know the behavior of such contaminants, monitoring systems to know the spatial distribution of contaminants, numerical simulations for prediction and remediation techniques for better solution.

Evaluation of underground environment change in urban area and possibility of heat island improvement by "surplus" groundwater

In urban area, groundwater is used for life and industry. However the groundwater pumping with no plan can cause the problems such as land subsidence, oxygen defficient air, effects on underground infrastructure, etc. On the other hand, heat island is recognised as significant issue in urban area. To solve these problrms at one time, we consider pumping groundwater with "feasible" rate and introducing the groundwater into pavements to cool the surface environment are effective. We are developping techniques to estimate groundwater level that does not cause the land sumsidence, to realize and keep it, to evaluate possible groundwater pumping rate and to evaluate the effects of available groundwater for heat island mitigation.

Development and application of techniques to predict long-term materials transportation in the Geosphere

Radioactive wastes from atomic power plants are finally disposed in the deep underground. It takes tens of thousand years until the radioactivity of those wastes decreases to the level of natural uranium. We have to choose the disposal site that can seal the radioactive wastes for such years. One possible system to bring the radioactive wastes out from the disposal site is material transportation due to groundwater flow. It is important to confirm that the groundwater does not flow in the disposal site. We are developing the techniques to verify that the diffusion dominates the material transportation in the site by stable isotope analysis.

Research on development of sedimentary basin and pore-fluid flow through geological time scale

To evaluate the generation of oil & gas field, it is necessary to understand the development processes of sedimentary basin and the behavior of pore-fluid flow through geological time scale. We are developing the techniques to simulate how geological impacts affect on geosphere environment and how pore-fluids flow.
