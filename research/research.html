Evaluation of groundwater environment and its long-term stability in coastal area

Multidisciplinary approach has been taken to evaluate the stability of surface and subsurface environments in a long term (ca. 10,000 years). The disciplines are topography, geology, hydrology, archaeology, etc. For example, we developed techniques to reconstruct glacial sea level change from records on sediment in high resolution, in the field. We succeeded in observing the quantity and quality of Submarine Groundwater Discharge (SGD). We are trying to evaluate the groundwater systems from inland to ocean.

Development of methods for evaluating the physical properties of underground formations from observation on natural fractuation

It is important to obtain the physical property of underground materials for sustainable development. For this evaluation, we are developing the techniques from the view point of following ideas. Geosphere responses to natural fractuation such as tidal loading, atmospheric pressure change and seasonal recharge change, etc. Many studies considered these signals as the noise against the focused phenomena and removed them for their analysis. However, there is the probability to evaluate the physical properties of underground materials under the natural condition by analyzing these signals with poromechanics theory. Now we are developing and applying the observation equipments for this approach in the several fields. These approach are expected to become one of the general methods to evaluate the physical properties of underground materials under the natural condition.

Evaluation of groundwater contamination and development of remediation techniques

Contamination problems of groundwater and soils are big issues in materializing the sustainable society. Especially, the contamination by hydrocarbon or chlorohydrocarbon are important. We are developing the experiments and monitoring systems to depict the behavior of such contaminants, and the spatial distribution of contaminant. We are also try to establish numerical simulations to design prediction and remediation techniques for better resolutions.

Evaluation of underground environment change in urban area and possibility of heat island improvement by "surplus" groundwater

Groundwater, in urban area, is used for life and industry. Groundwater pumping with no plan can cause problems such as land subsidence, oxygen-deficient air, adverse effects on underground infrastructure, etc. In addition, heat island has been recognised as significant issue in urban area. To solve these problems simultaneously, we pump out groundwater with "feasible" rate and introduce the groundwater into pavements to cool the surface environment. We develop techniques to estimate the groundwater level that does not cause the land subsidence, to realize and keep it, and to evaluate possible groundwater pumping rate and the effects of available groundwater for heat island mitigation.

Development and application of techniques to predict long-term materials transportation in the Geosphere

Radioactive wastes from atomic power plants are finally disposed in the deep underground. It takes tens of thousand years until the radioactivity of those wastes decreases to the level of natural uranium. We have to choose the disposal site that can seal the radioactive wastes for such years. One possible process to bring the radioactive wastes out from the disposal site is material transportation due to groundwater flow. It is important to confirm that the groundwater does not flow in the disposal site. We are developing the techniques to verify that the diffusion dominates the material transportation in the site by stable isotope analysis.

Research on development of sedimentary basin and pore-fluid flow through geological time scale

To evaluate the generation of oil & gas field, it is necessary to understand the development processes of sedimentary basin and the behavior of pore-fluid flow in the geological time scale. We are developing the techniques to simulate how geological impacts affect on geosphere environment and how pore-fluids flow.
