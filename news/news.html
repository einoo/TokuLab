Field excursion to Sado Island, Niigata Prefecture, 12-13, November 2018

Totally 18 members went to Sado Island, Niigata Prefecture for the field excursion during 12th and 13th, November 2018.

Sado Island is one of the largest island in Japan. It is famous for its gold and silver mine, which we have visited during this field excursion. We have closely looked at the pillow shape basalt, which is formed through rapid heating and cooling during the flow, on the offshore of Japan Sea.

On the other day, we have visited the natural gas and oil company in Niigata station. The special geology formation in Niigata prefecture and the related environmental considerations, such as land subsidence, are investigated.

2018 GSA Bridsall Dreiss Distinguished Lecture

In 2018, the Birdsall-Driess Distinguished lecture was selected by GSA: The GEological Society of America. Associate Professor David Bouutt from Department of Geosciences, University of Massachusetts-Amherst would give a lecture on "The role of inter-basin groundwater flow and hydrogeological processes" at Kashiwa Campus, The University of Tokyo.
